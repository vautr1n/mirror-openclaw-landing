The Openclaw ban, explained

Anthropic banned Openclaw. Now what?

Openclaw was a weekend project that gave Claude the one thing it didn't have: your memory. Thousands of users wired it up — scraping notes, piping in emails, stitching personal context into every chat — because the alternative was an AI that reset to zero every morning. It worked. Until Anthropic banned it because the way it injected context fell outside their platform policies.

The ban killed the tool. It did not kill the problem. Every AI on the market resets to zero every morning. They're trained on the average of humanity, not on you. Not on your relationships, your commitments, or the history of what you actually care about. No bolt-on hack was ever going to fix that. The fix has to be a layer. A properly built layer. And that layer should have existed from day one.

Frequently asked

About the Openclaw ban, and about Mirror.

Why was Openclaw banned by Anthropic?

Openclaw was removed from the Claude ecosystem because the way it injected user context into Claude fell outside Anthropic's platform policies. The ban is about how context was delivered, not about whether users should have personal context. The underlying need has not gone away.

What is a personal AI memory layer?

A persistent, secure system that stores who you are — your relationships, topics, ongoing projects, commitments, and history — and makes that knowledge available to the AI tools you use, so they stop starting from zero every morning.

Is Mirror just "Openclaw 2.0"?

No. Openclaw was a technology — flat text memory, a weekend of setup, constant effort, welded to one model. Mirror is a product — a knowledge graph of people and topics and commitments, proactive by default, working out of the box, built with platform compliance in mind from day one.

Does Mirror replace ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini?

No. Mirror is complementary. ChatGPT, Claude and Gemini are open-ended playgrounds — brilliant, but built on the average of humanity, not on you. Mirror is the personal layer beside them: the memory, the context, the history they were never built with.

Will Mirror get banned too?

We designed for that possibility from day one. Mirror doesn't modify or intercept third-party AI apps. Your context lives on your side of the wall and compounds over time. We are building this to last.

Is Mirror secure?

Institutional-grade security is the foundation, not an afterthought. Encrypted in transit and at rest today, on-device architecture on the roadmap. No training on your private data. Domain separation between work and personal context. Full ownership, consent, traceability, auditability.

What does Mirror actually see?

Only what you choose to connect. Email and calendar at launch — rich enough on their own to deliver real value. Notes, meeting transcripts, and messaging apps next. You can disconnect anything at any time, and domain separation prevents work context from leaking into personal queries or vice versa.
